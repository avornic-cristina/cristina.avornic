Profile

The person
behind the protocol

I have spent 14 years making complex, high-stakes events run without a visible seam. That is what protocol is: the infrastructure that lets diplomacy happen. When it works, nobody notices. When it doesn't, everyone does.

My career has taken me from Chisinau to Brussels to Geneva, through national ministries, a permanent EU mission, and four years at the Permanent Mission to the UN, WTO and international organisations in Geneva — the same diplomatic ecosystem CERN operates in.

I have coordinated 150+ representational events involving Heads of State, Ministers, and Ambassadors. I have advised senior leadership on precedence and protocol under pressure. I have negotiated treaty positions on behalf of 19 states. And I have done it in four languages.

Why this role,
at this organisation

CERN is a unique institution — a global scientific hub operating under host-state agreements with 23 member states, managing senior diplomatic visits with the same precision it applies to its physics experiments. That environment is exactly where I work best.

I have spent four years in Geneva's multilateral ecosystem — the same environment where CERN operates. I understand Swiss protocol requirements, the dynamics of international delegations visiting a host-state institution, and the operational complexity of coordinating visits where political, scientific, and diplomatic dimensions all intersect.

CERN does not need someone who will learn on the job. It needs someone who arrives on day one with the cross-institutional coordination experience, the protocol advisory background, and the composure under last-minute changes that this role demands. That is the career I have built.
